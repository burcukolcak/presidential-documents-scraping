EXECUTIVE ORDER 8278
WITHDRAWING PUBLIC LAND AND WATER FOR NAVAL PURPOSES
October 28, 1939
ALASKA
By virtue of and pursuant to the authority vested in me by the act of June 25, 1910, c. 421, 36 Stat. 847, as amended by the act of August 24, 1912, c. 369, 37 Stat. 497, and subject to the conditions therein expressed, it is ordered as follows:
SECTION 1. The public land and water in the following described area in the Territory of Alaska are hereby withdrawn from settlement, location, sale, or entry and reserved for the use of the Department of the Navy for naval purposes:
The eastern portion of Kodiak Island, described by metes and bounds as follows:
Beginning at a point at latitude 57 degrees 47'0" north, longitude 152 degrees 26'30" west, thence:
W. to latitude 57 degrees 47'0' north, longitude 152 degrees 36'0" west
S. to latitude 57 degrees 44'30" north, longitude 152 degrees 38'0 " west
SW. to latitude 57 degrees 42'0" north, longitude 152 degrees 38'0 " west
S. to latitude 57 degrees 39'30" north, longitude 152 degrees 38'0 " west
E. to latitude 57 degrees 39'30" north, longitude 152 degrees 30'0 " west
NE. to latitude 57 degrees 42'0" north, longitude 152 degrees 26'0 " west
N. to latitude 57 degrees 44'0" north, longitude 152 degrees 26'0" west
NW. to latitude 57 degrees 47'0" north, longitude 152 degrees 26'30 " west
to the point of beginning.
SECTION 2. This order supersedes Executive Order No. 7748 [FN1] of November 20, 1937, reserving a certain area in Alaska for the use of the Navy Department for naval purposes.
SECTION 3. The reservation made by section 1 of this order shall remain in force until revoked by the President or by act of Congress.
FRANKLIN D. ROOSEVELT

THE WHITE HOUSE,

October 28, 1939.
