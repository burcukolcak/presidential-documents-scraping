George W. Bush
July 23, 2008

Message to the Congress on Continuation of the National Emergency Regarding Export Control Regulations
To the Congress of the United States:
Section 202(d) of the National Emergencies Act (50 U.S.C. 1622(d)), provides for the automatic termination of a national emergency unless, prior to the anniversary date of its declaration, the President publishes in the Federal Register and transmits to the Congress a notice stating that the emergency is to continue in effect beyond the anniversary date. In accordance with this provision, I have sent to theFederal Registerfor publication the enclosed notice, stating that the emergency caused by the lapse of the Export Administration Act of 1979, as amended, is to continue in effect for 1 year beyond August 17, 2008.
GEORGE W. BUSH
The White House, July 23, 2008.
NOTE: This message was released by the Office of the Press Secretary on July 24. The notice of July 23 is listed in Appendix D at the end of this volume.
